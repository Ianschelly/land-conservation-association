Holly Gibbs

FOUNDER AND CO-DIRECTOR
Email
Personal site

Dr. Holly Gibbs is a professor at the University of Wisconsin-Madison where she directs a large team of scientists and students in the Global Land Use and Environment Lab (www.gibbs-lab.com). She is a Land System Scientist who studies how and why people use land around the world and what these changes mean for the future of our planet. She was trained as a physical scientist and focuses on interdisciplinary, applied questions around human-environment interactions, globalization, environment, and policy. Gibbs and her team have developed novel methods to map commodity supply chains in support of company and government actions to reduce tropical deforestation, as well as carbon stocks and emissions. She works in Brazil, Colombia, Peru and in the United States where she is spinning up an agenda focused on the importance of land conservation on private properties.

Gibbs was selected as the Piers J. Sellers Global Environmental Change award by the American Geophysical Union in 2024 and was identified by the Web of Science as a High Cited Researcher ranking in the top 1% by citations for their field(s) and publication year over the past decade - indicating exceptional influence in their respective disciplines. Gibbs has previously received the Romnes Fellowship, Kellet Mid-Career Award, the Chancellor's Distinguished Teaching Award, Dean's Award for Distinguished Faculty Achievement, Post-Doctoral Association’s Mentorship Award, OECD Research Fellowship, and Smith Conservation Research Fellowship, among others.
